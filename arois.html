Aerial Robotic Optical Identification System

During the Fall of 2015 I began work with a team on a drone aircraft that could perform sense-and-avoid operations in flight. Besides working on the flight dynamics of the aircraft, I also began to develop the software and embedded systems on the craft to provide it with autonomous capabilities.


The aircraft was developed on a budget of approximately $500, and I was allotted approximately 20% of that budget to provide the sensors and hardware necessary to implement the sense-and-avoid system.


The system was run on a Raspberry Pi 2 Model B and written in Python. Multiple threads pulled images in realtime from a USB web camera mounted on the wing of the aircraft and analyzed using OpenCV.

After numerous lab tests, the system finally achieved proof-of-concept on its third flight test, achieving aerial detection of objects on both a LIDARlite v2 and via computer vision from onboard cameras
