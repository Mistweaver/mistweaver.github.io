Who I am

"He's like a nice, athletic frat guy, but a total nerd." - sister-in-law of an ex-girlfriend.

I have always taken great pride in being a multi-faceted individual, whether that means being an athlete studying math and sciences or confusing my college advisors why I would mix biology courses into my engineering curriculum.

This has been a guiding principle that I continue to carry into my work. I don't believe in limiting one's scope of knowledge, personality, or experiences. The world and the problems we face are too complex to be solved from a singular view or attitude.

While studying aerospace engineering at Purdue University and playing collegiate rugby, I became fascinated with software, robotics, and how they could be seamlessly blended daily lives. I believe a harmony between technology and nature not only can be found, but might be the key to the future.

And if I'm wrong, then at least I'll be having fun doing so.

Whats with the glowing lights?

Reverse engineering the concept for the Playstation 2 menu, the lights grouping represents the lowest common denominator (LCD) of the number 60 throughout the course of one minute. There are 6 possible combinations of orb groupings in relation to the number of seconds elapsed at that moment, in this case the number of seconds in one minute
